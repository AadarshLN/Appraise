• Higlighting errors:
    • Highlight the text fragment where you have identified a translation error with your cursor or finger.
    • Click repeatedly on the highlighted fragment to increase its severity level or to remove the selection. The available severity levels:
    • Severity:
      • Major: Seriously changed meaning, difficult to read, decreases usability.
      • Minor: Style/grammar/lexical choice could be better/more natural.
    • If something is missing from the text, mark it as an error on the [MISSING] word.
  • Score: Highlighting more errors or changing their severity levels automatically updates the translation quality score on the slider. After highlighting all errors, before continuing to the next screen, please manually update this score to better reflect your judgement. The quality levels associated with numerical scores on the slider:
    • 0: No meaning preserved: Nearly all information is lost in the translation.
    • 33%: Some meaning preserved: The translation preserves some of the meaning but misses significant parts. The narrative is hard to follow due to errors. Grammar may be poor.
    • 66%: Most meaning preserved and few grammar mistakes: The translation retains most of the meaning. It may have some grammar mistakes or minor inconsistencies.
    • 100%: Perfect meaning and grammar: The meaning and grammar of the translation is completely consistent with the source.
